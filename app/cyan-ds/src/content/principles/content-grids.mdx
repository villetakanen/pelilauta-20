# Content Grids

Content Grids are the structural backbone of Cyan. They prioritize readability and deterministic spacing by utilizing a 3-column "Holy Grail" grid applied globally to `section` and `article` tags within the main content area.

## Structural Logic

All content regions respond to the width of the parent `<main>` container using **Container Queries**.

- **Mobile (< 60rem):** Gutter padding of `--cn-gap` (16px) is enforced.
- **Desktop (>= 60rem):** Content is centered with a max-width of **`67ch`** (the optimal character measure for focused reading).

### Default Centering

By default, all children of a `section` or `article` are anchored to the central column.

<div class="cn-grid-debug" style="background: var(--cn-surface-1); border: 1px dashed var(--cn-border-color); padding: 1rem;">
  <section class="cn-content-grid" style="background: var(--cn-surface-2); border: 1px solid var(--cn-color-primary-low); position: relative;">
    <small style="position: absolute; top: 4px; right: 8px; opacity: 0.5; font-size: 10px;">Grid Column 2</small>
    <h3>Standard Section</h3>
    <p>This section is automatically centered to exactly 67ch on wide screens. No CSS classes were applied to this paragraph or header to achieve this alignment.</p>
  </section>
</div>


## The Full-Width Bleed

When an element needs to break the 67ch constraint (e.g. for banners, large tables, or full-width imagery), use the `.cn-grid-full` class.

<div class="cn-grid-debug" style="background: var(--cn-surface-1); border: 1px dashed var(--cn-border-color); padding: 1rem; margin-top: 2rem;">
  <section class="cn-content-grid" style="background: var(--cn-surface-2); border: 1px solid var(--cn-color-primary-low);">
    <p>I am centered (67ch).</p>
    <div class="cn-grid-full" style="background: var(--cn-color-primary-low); padding: 1rem; color: white; text-align: center;">
      <b>I am .cn-grid-full</b>
      <br/>(I span the entire width including the margins)
    </div>
    <p>I am centered again.</p>
  </section>
</div>


## Vertical Rhythm

Direct siblings are automatically separated by `2 * --cn-gap` to maintain constant typographical density without manual margin management.
